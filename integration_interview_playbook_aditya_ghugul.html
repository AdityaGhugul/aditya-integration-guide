Why this guide exists

I had the experience. I just couldn't explain it.

After 5 years in integration engineering — 3 of them specializing in Axway APIM at TCS handling over 100 million API requests per day — I hit a frustrating wall.

The job calls were rare. And when they came, I knew the answers. I had built complex OAuth flows, designed policy-based security architectures, debugged production incidents at scale. But in interviews, I stumbled.

"I knew how to do the work. I just couldn't say it in the language interviewers expected."

I'd answer in layman terms when I needed technical precision. I'd describe what I built without naming the patterns. I knew content-based routing — I just called it "checking the message and deciding where to send it."

So I built this. 100 questions organized exactly the way a real interviewer thinks — from instant-rejection basics to architect-level system design. 100 terms with the exact interview-ready sentences to use them in. And a 10-week plan that fits around a full-time job.

This guide is for every integration engineer who has done real work but struggles to present it in the language that gets you hired.

Getting started

How to use this guide

This is not a document to read once. It is a training system. Use it in short daily sessions for maximum retention.

01
Start with the red tier
The 5 instant-rejection questions are non-negotiable. If you can't answer these cold, nothing else matters. Master them first.
02
Click to reveal answers
Try to answer every question out loud before clicking. Speaking forces recall. Reading is passive. Interviews are spoken.
03
Use the terminology cards
For each term, read your layman version first — you already know this. Then read the technical version. Then say the interview sentence out loud 3 times.
04
10 questions per day
Do not binge. 10 questions per day, spoken aloud, is more effective than 100 questions read silently in one sitting.
05
Map to your own stories
For every question you can answer technically, immediately think of a real project where you did that. That becomes your interview story.
06
Apply from week 3
Don't wait until you feel "ready." Apply from week 3 for stretch roles. Real interviews teach you more than solo practice ever will.

Preparation roadmap

10-Week Study Plan

Designed for someone with a full-time job — 1.5 to 2 hours per day maximum. Consistent beats intensive every time.

WEEK 1
Foundation Pass — Questions 1–50
Go through the first 50 questions. Don't memorize — understand. Mark every question you can't answer confidently. Build your gap list.
Daily: 1hr questions + 30min gap notes
WEEK 2
Foundation Pass — Questions 51–100
Complete the second 50 questions. By end of week you should have a clear picture of which domains are strong and which need work.
Daily: 1hr questions + 30min gap notes
WEEK 3
Gap Closing — Weakest Domain
Deep dive your weakest domain only. If OAuth is weak, read RFC 6749. If Kafka is unfamiliar, build a tiny demo. Fix gaps the questions exposed.
Daily: 1.5hr focused domain study
WEEK 4
Gap Closing — Second Weakest Domain
Repeat for your second weakest domain. Don't spread thin — go deep on one thing at a time. Quality of understanding beats breadth of coverage.
Daily: 1.5hr focused domain study
WEEK 5
Terminology — 10 Terms Per Day
Work through the terminology cheat sheet. For each term: say layman version → say technical version → say interview sentence out loud. Record yourself.
Daily: 30min terms (spoken, not read)
WEEK 6
Your Story Bank
Write 10 real stories from your work using STAR format. Cover: complex design, security challenge, production incident, performance problem, stakeholder conflict, migration.
Daily: 45min writing + refining stories
WEEK 7
System Design Practice
Pick 2 expert-tier questions per day. Spend 20 minutes designing on paper before reading the answer. Do this out loud. Draw it. Narrate it.
Daily: 1hr design practice (spoken)
WEEK 8
System Design Practice + Apply
Continue design practice. Start applying to 2–3 roles per week. Early interviews are the best practice. A week-4 rejection teaches more than solo study.
Daily: 45min design + applications
WEEK 9
Second Pass + Mock Interviews
Second pass of all 100 questions — much faster now. Focus on ones you still stumble on. Do mock interviews with a peer if possible. Debrief after each.
Daily: 1hr revision + active applying
WEEK 10
Final Polish + Active Interview Mode
Light revision only. Focus energy on real interviews. Every interview gives you a data point. Track what was asked, review after, update your weak areas list.
Daily: 30min light prep + interviews
⚡ The Most Important Mindset Shift

Stop thinking of technical terms as "fancy words for simple things." Start thinking of them as compressed, precise language that signals you think at the same level as the interviewer. When you say "content-based routing" instead of "checking the message to decide where to send it" — you're not showing off. You're demonstrating that you belong in the conversation. That's all an interview is.
